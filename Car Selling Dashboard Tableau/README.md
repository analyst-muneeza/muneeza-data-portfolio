# Car Selling Dashboard using Tableau

This project presents an interactive Car Selling Dashboard created in Tableau to analyze car sales trends, selling prices, ownership patterns, fuel types, and vehicle distribution over the years.
The dashboard helps in understanding market trends and identifying factors affecting car selling prices.

📌 Project Objective

The main objective of this project is to:

Analyze car selling prices based on fuel type and ownership
Study yearly trends in car listings and prices
Compare selling price with kilometers driven
Perform Pareto and growth analysis
Build interactive visualizations for better business insights

📊 Dashboard Visualizations
1. Fuel-wise Average Selling Price Analysis

Bar chart showing average selling price for different fuel types.

2. Owner-wise Price vs Km Driven Analysis

Dual axis chart comparing selling price and average kilometers driven by ownership type.

3. Year-wise Average Selling Price Trend

Line chart representing changes in average selling price over the years.

4. Fuel Set Comparison Analysis

Set-based bar chart comparing selected fuel categories.

5. Car Brand Group Selling Price Analysis

Grouped bar chart comparing selling prices of car brand groups.

6. Dynamic Parameter-Based Selling Price Analysis

Interactive parameter chart allowing dynamic field selection.

7. Year-wise Car Listings Distribution

Bar chart displaying number of listed cars each year.

8. Owner-wise Pareto Analysis of Selling Price

Pareto chart identifying major contributors to overall selling price.

9. Fuel-wise Car Count Distribution

Bar chart showing distribution of cars across fuel types.

10. Year-wise Car Listings Trend

Line chart representing trend of car listings over time.

11. Year-over-Year Growth Rate Analysis

Line chart analyzing yearly percentage growth in car listings.

12. Owner-wise Calculated Metric Comparison

Bar chart comparing calculated metrics across ownership categories.

📈 Key Insights
Diesel cars have the highest average selling price.
Selling price generally decreases as ownership count increases.
Car listings significantly increased after 2010.
Petrol and Diesel vehicles dominate the market.
Year-over-year growth fluctuates across different periods.
Test drive cars show higher average selling prices.
